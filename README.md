# Muslimah Sarumi — Product Portfolio

A personal portfolio exploring product thinking, systems design, and experiments at the intersection of software, AI, and human behaviour.

## About

I'm a Product Manager with a background in software engineering. I care about how systems actually work in the real world, especially where technology meets messy human behaviour.

This portfolio is a collection of products, case studies, and small experiments I've built while exploring questions around usability, accessibility, AI-assisted workflows, and service design.

Some of these projects are production work. Others are prototypes and experiments designed to test an idea or understand a problem better.

## What's Inside

The site is structured around four areas:

**Product Work**
A selection of products I've worked on or designed, including SaaS platforms, AI prototypes, and workflow systems.

**Case Studies**
Expanded breakdowns of selected projects, covering problem framing, research, product decisions, and outcomes.

**Experiments**

Essays on product thinking, systems design, and what it actually takes to build things that work.
## Selected Projects

**NeuroStudy Buddy**  
AI-powered study assistant designed to simplify dense academic text for students with dyslexia and ADHD.

**Enterprise Client Onboarding**  
Workflow redesign that improved how a digital marketing company captured and structured client onboarding information.

**ClearPath**  
A citizen-first GovTech concept platform designed to simplify access to social services using GDS-aligned service design principles.

**CoreTel**  
A SaaS telecom operations dashboard designed to streamline subscription management, SIP provisioning, and reseller billing workflows.

## Why This Portfolio Exists

I built this portfolio as a place to document how I think about product.

Not just the outcomes, but the reasoning behind decisions, the constraints, the tradeoffs, and the experiments that shaped them.

Product work is often invisible. This is my attempt to make some of that thinking visible.
